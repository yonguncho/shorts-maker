# Hook Frameworks for US-Stock Shorts

Category: hook_formulas
Source: internal-knowledge-seed
Rules: < 12 words, English, question-form or pattern-interrupt preferred. Keep hedging. Never command buy/sell. First 3 words carry the stop.

## Construction rules
A hook does three jobs in under 2 seconds: deliver a concrete number or claim, open a curiosity gap, and signal stakes. Front-load the strongest token (a percentage, a ticker, or a contrarian verb) into the first three words because the feed shows the opening instant as a live thumbnail. Avoid setup clauses ("Today we look at...") — start at the payoff fragment.

## Curiosity Gap (Hormozi)
Pattern: "Did X — but the result was Y, and the reason is Z (revealed later)."
fit_for: surprise present, indirect catalyst. avg_retention_lift: high.
- "ARM ripped 15% — and earnings had NOTHING to do with it."
- "TSM jumped 8% today. The reason is 3,000 miles away."
- "This chip stock doubled. Its biggest customer never noticed."
Anti-pattern: stating the reason in the hook itself (closes the loop too early).

## Negation Hook
Pattern: "Everyone says X. The data says otherwise."
fit_for: overbought/oversold debate, consensus mismatch. avg_retention_lift: high.
- "Everyone says NVDA is overbought. The volume disagrees."
- "They call it a bubble. The order book doesn't."
- "Wall Street downgraded it. Funds kept buying."
Anti-pattern: negating something nobody actually believes (no tension).

## Number Specificity
Pattern: "Only N% know / N traders missed X."
fit_for: chart insight, unusual data. avg_retention_lift: medium-high.
- "Only 3% of traders watched THIS chart yesterday."
- "9 out of 10 missed why AMD lagged today."
Anti-pattern: round, vague numbers ("a lot of people") — specificity is the credibility.

## Time Pressure
Pattern: "In 60 seconds: X."
fit_for: explainer, comparison. avg_retention_lift: medium.
- "In 60 seconds: why TSM moved more than ARM."
- "60 seconds to understand today's biggest AI-chip move."

## Pattern Interrupt
Pattern: a statement opposite to the expected takeaway.
fit_for: overheated names, risk emphasis. avg_retention_lift: high.
- "ARM is up 15%. Don't chase it yet."
- "Great earnings. The chart should scare you."
Anti-pattern: interrupt with no follow-through (must be justified in the body).

## Authority Reference
Pattern: "When {institution} does X, this follows."
fit_for: analyst_action, smart-money catalyst. avg_retention_lift: medium-high.
- "When Morgan Stanley raises a PT 40%, this usually follows."
- "Goldman just flagged this chip name. Here's the read."

## Open Loop
Pattern: promise an answer revealed at the end.
fit_for: lagging/related plays, payoff structure. avg_retention_lift: high.
- "By the end, you'll know the ONE chip stock that lagged."
- "Stay for the name nobody is watching yet."

## Mistake / Warning
Pattern: "The mistake everyone makes with X."
fit_for: risk scene emphasis, overextension. avg_retention_lift: medium-high.
- "The mistake traders make chasing a 52-week high."
- "Buying the breakout? Watch this first."

## Contrarian Data
Pattern: lead with a stat that contradicts the headline narrative.
fit_for: volume/RSI divergences. avg_retention_lift: high.
- "Stock up 15%, volume says it's fragile."
- "New highs — on the weakest volume in a month."

## Before / After (transformation)
Pattern: compress a dramatic change into a timeframe.
fit_for: fast multi-day moves. avg_retention_lift: medium-high.
- "From 52-week low to high in 3 weeks. How?"
- "Flat for a year. Then 4 days changed everything."

## Stakes / consequence
Pattern: imply who wins or loses from the move.
fit_for: value-chain, sympathy plays. avg_retention_lift: medium.
- "ARM just won. Three suppliers just got a problem."
- "One chip launch, two stocks crushed."

## Question stack
Pattern: a single sharp question the viewer also wants answered.
fit_for: "am I too late" anxiety. avg_retention_lift: high.
- "ARM at all-time highs — too late to care?"
- "Is this an AI breakout or a volume trap?"

## Power verbs and tokens
Stop-words that earn attention: ripped, exploded, crushed, quietly, secretly, nobody, only, why, before. Use sparingly and truthfully; an exaggerated verb on a small move trains distrust. A real +15% can carry "ripped"; a +2% move cannot.

## Hedged hook discipline
Even a punchy hook keeps the channel's hedged posture intact. The hook raises a question; the body answers with "likely/appears/could". Never let the hook assert a price prediction or a buy/sell. Curiosity and stakes create the stop, not certainty.
