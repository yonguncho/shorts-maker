# US Market Domain Knowledge

Category: us_market
Source: internal-knowledge-seed
Use only as interpretive framework; all numbers come from yfinance, all claims stay hedged.

## Catalyst taxonomy and durability
- earnings_beat: reported EPS/revenue above consensus. Fundamental, trend-capable; often DURABLE if guidance also holds.
- guidance_raise: forward outlook lifted. The strongest durable signal — the market pays for the future, not the past.
- analyst_action: upgrade or price-target raise. Name the firm and the old→new PT. Medium durability; fades if not confirmed by fundamentals.
- product_news / deal / contract: launch, design win, partnership. Durability depends on whether it converts to revenue; a "validation" move without revenue is often TEMPORARY.
- macro: rates, CPI, jobs, FOMC. Moves the whole tape; stock-specific edge is low. Label clearly as macro-driven.
- sector_sympathy / momentum: moved because a peer moved. Usually TEMPORARY with fade risk; the stock has no catalyst of its own.

## Durable vs temporary heuristics
DURABLE markers: own earnings/guidance, raised estimates, broad-based volume, multi-day follow-through. TEMPORARY markers: indirect catalyst (another company's news), single-day spike, low or fading volume, already extended (RSI>75, far above 52w trend). State the verdict with a hedge ("likely temporary").

## Index proxies and relative strength
SPY/S&P 500 = broad market, QQQ = Nasdaq-100 (tech-heavy), DIA = Dow, IWM = small caps. Always compare a single stock's move to SPY/QQQ for the day. "+15% vs SPX +0.3%" is real relative strength; "+1% on a +1% tape" is just beta.

## Key analysts and institutions
Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, Bank of America, Citi, Barclays, Wedbush (Dan Ives on tech), Bernstein, Mizuho. A price-target change or rating move from these is a smart-money catalyst; cite firm + old PT → new PT + date. Treat as a signal to observe, not advice.

## Options unusual activity (interpretation)
Large call (or put) volume relative to open interest, sharp IV spikes, and sweeps indicate fast positioning. Bullish flow = heavy call buying near-the-money with rising IV; bearish = put accumulation. Always frame as positioning to observe; never as a recommendation, and always hedge.

## Volume authenticity rule
Compare today's volume to the 30-day average. ≥1.5× = conviction (real participation behind the move); 1.0–1.5× = neutral; <1.0× = suspect (the move may fade). Volume confirmation is the line between a durable breakout and a low-quality pop.

## Chart-position reads ("am I too late?")
Near or above the 52-week high = breakout (momentum, but chase risk — poor risk/reward without a pullback). Recovering off the lows = early-stage, more room. RSI > 70 = overbought / overextended; RSI < 30 = oversold. Count consecutive up days; 4+ in a row plus RSI>75 = stretched. Always answer the viewer's real question directly.

## Earnings reaction patterns
Beat-and-raise → strongest, often gaps and holds. Beat-but-guide-down → "sell the news," a red flag even on a good print. In-line → muted. Miss-but-raise → mixed, watch guidance. The guidance matters more than the headline EPS for durability.

## Short squeeze and float dynamics
High short interest (% of float) + a positive catalyst + thin float can amplify a move beyond fundamentals. Note it as a mechanical amplifier, not a thesis. Low float makes moves violent in both directions.

## Macro calendar awareness
CPI, PCE, jobs report (NFP), and FOMC decisions move the whole market and can swamp single-stock catalysts. If a stock moved on a macro day, attribute carefully; the move may be beta, not news. Earnings season clusters reactions by sector.

## Leveraged single-stock ETFs
NVDX/NVDL (2× NVDA), TSLL (2× TSLA), AAPU (2× AAPL), etc. amplify the underlying's daily move ~2×. Useful as an eye-catching "amplified" data point, but they are derivative and decay; never present them as the related-stock analysis, and always note the leverage.

## Sector rotation and breadth
Money rotates between sectors (e.g., into semis on an AI theme, out of defensives). Breadth — how many names in a theme are up — signals whether a move is a single name or a whole group. "8 of 11 AI-chip names higher" is a stronger theme signal than one stock alone.
